How to work

We believe that teamwork ought to be energizing, supportive, and engaging. Working in teams on a project should be something that we all get huge value from. Here is some guidance on how to work effectively as a team -

Hard Work is Not Directly Relevant

We don't measure the success of team members based on how many weekends or nights they work. For us performance is about your effectiveness; even though effectiveness is much harder to measure. We try to measure people based on how quickly and how well they get their work done, especially under tight deadlines.

Availability and Communication

We encourage team members to be available for communication (via video or on Slack) as much as they wish and feel is appropriate for their current task. Communication, as they say, is the key to every relationship. Working remotely makes synchronous communication a choice, not a requirement. While the benefits of being able to "go dark" and focus on a task without risk of distraction are great, we must also make time to be explicitly available to the team.

Radical Candor

Having a great relationship as a team also requires us to be able to give and receive a lot of feedback. As such, we follow the Radical Candor methodology to give feedback to each other which in turn helps everyone achieve their fullest potential and drive results collaboratively.

Please familiarize yourself with the concept here. Radical Candor basically means the ability to Challenge Directly and show you Care Personally at the same time. Being Radically Candid means:

  • Being more specific and sincere with praise
  • Being more kind and clear with criticism

How do we optimize team performance?

Our ultimate goal is to create - high performance, high trust teams that are able to create amazing programs. We believe that the biggest impact on the performance of any organization is the quality of their team. Google did a project a couple of years back to understand the five key dynamics that set successful teams apart from other teams.

Here's what they discovered and what we encourage you to implement on a daily basis:

1. Psychological safety: Can we take risks on this team without feeling insecure or embarrassed? 2. Dependability: Can we count on each other to do high quality work on time? 3. Structure & clarity: Are goals, roles, and execution plans on our team clear? 4. Meaning of work: Are we working on something that is personally important for each of us? 5.
