Which tools should I use for what?

Time and attention are sacred. As a result, a big part of collaborating effectively in a remote team is figuring out when to communicate with each other.

Match the message to the channel. ¬†

What you say can be delivered in a myriad of formats: Email, chat, video call, phone call... So to avoid the barrage of messages pelting others without any rhyme or reason, we wanted to make clear what message should be delivered in which channel. Often this is related to the type and urgency of a message. For instance, a direct message in Slack might insinuate that a message is urgent and should be responded to within the hour... versus something posting a message in a general channel might mean it doesn‚Äôt need to be looked at until the next day, if at all.
